**Memoir: A Seamstress's Journey**

When I reflect on the tapestry of my life, memories of my youth in Italy come rushing back. At just 20 years old, I stood on the brink of a monumental decision. Leaving my home was a bittersweet farewell, but the allure of new beginnings in America tugged at my heart. Excitement mingled with nerves as I embarked on this journey, one that promised opportunities but also challenges.

Arriving in America, I quickly realized that the language barrier would be one of my first hurdles. The unfamiliar sounds of English filled the air, and I felt lost in the clamor. But determination was my faithful companion. I enrolled in classes and seized every chance to practice speaking. The kindness of people I met along the way buoyed my spirits. Finding work as a seamstress in a local shop was a true blessing. Stitch by stitch, I started to weave a new life, feeling more settled and connected in my adopted home.

Working with fabric was a balm for my homesickness. It was a skill I had honed in Italy, and it offered comfort amid uncertainty. Over time, my confidence grew as my skills improved, and the shop owners took notice. I was given more responsibilities, becoming involved in decision-making, which was both empowering and exhilarating.

The late 70s ushered in a significant chapter of my life. By then, I was in my 30s, and the conditions at the shop had become quite strenuous. A sense of unrest brewed among us, and my colleagues turned to me for leadership. They recognized my experience and understanding of our struggles. Thus, I became a union representative, a role that allowed me to advocate for fair wages and better working conditions. It was a demanding endeavor, yet profoundly rewarding. Through negotiating and attending meetings, I gleaned invaluable lessons on leadership and the power of solidarity.

Negotiating with management was no small feat. Resistance was a constant, and progress often felt elusive. Balancing my duties as a seamstress with my responsibilities as a union rep was a delicate juggling act, one that sometimes left me weary. However, every small victory, such as a pay raise or improved work conditions, reaffirmed the worth of our efforts. I remember a particularly tense negotiation in 1982 when the shop owners aimed to cut our health benefits. Maintaining those benefits was crucial for many of us, and after weeks of arduous discussions, we emerged victorious with an agreement that not only preserved but enhanced our benefits. It was a moment of immense pride for everyone involved.

In time, a natural transition awaited me. After years of frontline advocacy, I turned to teaching. I began by offering small sewing classes in my community, eager to impart my knowledge in a nurturing environment. Watching students, especially young people and novices, discover the joy of creating something with their hands was deeply gratifying.

Looking back on my journey, I am filled with gratitude and pride. Each phase of my life presented its own blend of challenges and rewards. Immigration had its daunting moments, yet it opened doors I never imagined. Being a union representative taught me the power of standing up for what is right, while teaching has allowed me to share a piece of myself with others. My life's tapestry is rich indeed, woven with threads of perseverance and kindness.

Through my efforts as a union rep, I hope I contributed positively to my community, improving conditions for my colleagues in meaningful ways. Teaching sewing has been equally rewarding, as I've witnessed many gain confidence and joy from learning a new skill. It is heartwarming to see creations blossom under the students' hands, instilling in them a sense of pride.
